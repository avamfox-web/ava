Why I Love the Piano

The piano has always felt like a second language to me — one I can speak even when words fall short. There's something incredibly grounding about sitting at a keyboard and letting your fingers find the notes. Whether I'm working through a technical piece or just improvising, the piano brings me back to myself.

My absolute favorite piece is Nocturne Op. 9 No. 2 in E♭ Major by Frédéric Chopin. It's tender, melancholic, and impossibly beautiful — everything a piece of music should be.

My Piano Journey

Here's an overview of where I've been and what I've explored:

  1. Beginner Years
    • Learning hand position and posture
    • Simple scales and finger exercises
    • First songs: "Mary Had a Little Lamb," "Ode to Joy"
  2. Intermediate Stage
    • Sight-reading sheet music
    • Learning dynamics: piano, forte, crescendo
    • Exploring classical composers: Bach, Beethoven, Mozart
  3. Falling in Love with Chopin
    • Discovering the Nocturnes
    • Working on Op. 9 No. 2 — my favorite piece
    • Learning about Romantic era music history
  4. What I Want to Learn Next
    • Debussy's Clair de Lune
    • Improvisation and jazz harmony
    • Composing my own pieces
